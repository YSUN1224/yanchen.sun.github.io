About

I am an interdisciplinary environmental scientist working at the interface of environmental chemistry and microbiology. My research focuses on aquatic plastic pollution, the fate and transformation of emerging contaminants, environmental instrumentation, and sustainable waste-to-resource technologies.

My current work investigates the behavior, transformation, and environmental impacts of plastic-associated materials in aquatic systems while advancing analytical methods and instrumentation for emerging pollution challenges. I also study microbially mediated environmental processes, circular resource use, and the conversion of plastic waste into useful chemicals and energy.

My long-term goal is to establish an independent research program that integrates chemistry, microbiology, and engineering to address pollution across natural and engineered systems. Through collaborations with industry partners, I translate fundamental scientific insight into practical strategies for pollution control and the design of next-generation chemicals and materials.

Research

My research connects fundamental studies of contaminant transformation and microbial processes with scalable technologies for environmental monitoring, pollution mitigation, resource recovery, and waste valorization.

Theme 1

Plastic Degradation in Coastal Waters

I investigate the behavior, transformation, and environmental impacts of plastic-associated materials in aquatic systems, including how sunlight, temperature, and microbial communities shape persistence.

Theme 2

Emerging Contaminants & Instrumentation

I advance analytical methods and environmental instrumentation for emerging pollution challenges, with attention to contaminant fate, transport, and transformation.

Theme 3

Waste-to-Resource Technologies

I explore circular resource use and the conversion of plastic waste into useful chemicals and energy, linking pollution control with sustainable materials design.

Theme 4

Microbially Mediated Processes

I study how microbial communities mediate environmental transformations, including contaminant degradation, biogeochemical cycling, and greenhouse gas-relevant processes.
