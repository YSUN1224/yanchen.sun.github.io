## About

`I am an interdisciplinary environmental scientist working at the interface of environmental chemistry and microbiology. My research focuses on aquatic plastic pollution, the fate and transformation of emerging contaminants, environmental instrumentation, and sustainable waste-to-resource technologies.`

`My current work investigates the behavior, transformation, and environmental impacts of plastic-associated materials in aquatic systems while advancing analytical methods and instrumentation for emerging pollution challenges. I also study microbially mediated environmental processes, circular resource use, and the conversion of plastic waste into useful chemicals and energy.`

`My long-term goal is to establish an independent research program that integrates chemistry, microbiology, and engineering to address pollution across natural and engineered systems. Through collaborations with industry partners, I translate fundamental scientific insight into practical strategies for pollution control and the design of next-generation chemicals and materials.`

## Research

### What he does

`Dr. Yanchen Sun studies how chemical and microbial processes control the transformation, persistence, and environmental impacts of pollutants and greenhouse gases across aquatic, soil, and engineered systems. His research integrates environmental chemistry, microbiology, and engineering to address plastic degradation, emerging contaminant fate, waste-to-resource conversion, and microbially mediated biogeochemical processes.`

### Future Research Program

`Dr. Sun's future research program will develop predictive and experimentally grounded approaches for understanding pollutant transformation across natural and engineered systems. The program will focus on designing materials with reduced environmental persistence, advancing quantitative tools for biodegradation assessment, and identifying microbial controls on contaminant fate and greenhouse gas emissions.`

### Collaborative Translation

`Through interdisciplinary and industry-engaged collaborations, Dr. Sun connects fundamental environmental science with practical applications in sustainable materials design, environmental monitoring, pollution mitigation, resource recovery, and waste valorization.`

### Theme 1: Plastic Degradation and Lifetime Prediction in Aquatic Systems

`Plastic materials undergo physical, chemical, and biological transformations after entering freshwater and marine environments. Dr. Sun investigates the mechanisms and environmental controls that govern plastic degradation and lifetime, including the roles of sunlight, temperature, material properties, and microbial communities.`

`His work combines field and mesocosm incubations, mass-loss measurements, particle respirometry, isotope-based carbon tracing, and microbial community analysis to quantify material lifetime and identify the processes that control degradation.`

### Theme 2: Fate and Transport of Contaminants of Emerging Concern

`Contaminants of emerging concern can move, transform, and persist across interconnected natural and engineered systems. Dr. Sun examines how environmental conditions and physical-chemical processes influence contaminant fate, transport, transformation, and persistence.`

`This research supports improved prediction of pollutant behavior and informs strategies for monitoring, risk assessment, and pollution control.`

### Theme 3: Waste-to-Resource Technologies

`Waste streams can potentially be transformed into useful chemicals, materials, and energy. Dr. Sun explores waste-to-resource approaches that connect pollution control with circular resource use and sustainable materials design.`

`This work evaluates how waste conversion technologies can reduce environmental impacts while supporting the development of more sustainable chemical and material systems.`

### Theme 4: Instrumentation and Methodology Development

`Accurate measurements are essential for understanding pollutant transformation in complex environmental systems. Dr. Sun develops and applies analytical methods and environmental instrumentation to improve the measurement, characterization, and monitoring of emerging pollution challenges.`

`These approaches support quantitative analysis of degradation rates, respiration, carbon mineralization, contaminant transformation, and material persistence under environmentally relevant conditions.`

### Theme 5: Microbially Mediated Environmental Processes

`Microorganisms play central roles in contaminant degradation, carbon cycling, nutrient transformations, and greenhouse gas emissions. Dr. Sun studies how microbial communities mediate environmental processes in soils, sediments, and aquatic systems.`

`His work uses microbiology, stable isotope approaches, and omics-based tools to identify active microbial populations and link microbial activity to measurable environmental transformations, including greenhouse gas-relevant processes.`
